'''<dfn>[https://kit.co Kit.co]</dfn>''' is a silo for creating collections of affiliate links to products recommended by the creator, and can be linked to from blog posts or other media.

== IndieWeb Examples ==

* {{aaronpk}} linked to several kit.co kits from a blog post [https://aaronparecki.com/2020/03/24/4/tips-and-tools-for-remote-meetings-and-presentations Tips and Tools for Improving your Remote Meetings and Presentations on a Budget]

== Criticism ==

* 2021-09-13 [https://kit.co/blog/announcement-trusted-retailers-for-kit/ Kit.co announced "Trusted Retailers"] to cut down on spam on the website, which limits the domain names that can be used in product links to a list approved by Kit.co admins. At launch, only a small number of well-known store websites were supported. New websites of smaller online shops can be requested to be added through an online approval process.
