=== Silos ===
* Instagram posts are clipped by Are.na and then fetched on Tabitha's site (PESOS, [[PESOS)**]] https://www.are.na/, a site to save things, with a "quiet onboarding"
** It's noted the main feed has reduced in quality after recent growth
* For indicating the icon in the menu bar, use "alt" field
* Are.na has private / public sections and API
** Can export content on Are.na as PDF so content is not locked-in
* Difference between owning content vs location/hosting
** Neocities allow you to export all raw files, but hosting is managed by them, i.e., if you take it somewhere else, the links will break
* Q: Any good Silos in the present day? Has data portability / support exports, intra-operability
* Livejournal (back in the day)
* Mastodon is interesting because of federation; is it a true silo?
** If the instance suddenly shutdown, then all your content is lost; unless you run your own instance that you own (setup automated backup etc), so in that sense, that's a bad silo
* Is IndieWeb chat a silo? IRC is the source of truth and syndicated to other chat platforms
** There are mods, so sort of, but there's not much permanence, so more of a communication platform, but it is indeed "siloed" to Slack/Discord etc
** [[discuss]] Cory Doctorow: Early day on Facebook > respond to MySpace posts (intra-op example)
** Author who coined the term "enshittification", https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cory_Doctorow
** Weak company = more intra-op, attract more users. Company grown = lock people in!
** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embrace,_extend,_and_extinguish
* https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/danah_boyd 
** "Right to be forgotten"
