'''<dfn>[https://events.indieweb.org/2026/06/homebrew-website-club-nuremberg-germany-tXeSfxjMZdYt Homebrew Website Club Nürnberg]</dfn>''' was an IndieWeb meetup in real life that took place on 2026-06-10. The last event before this one was held on [https://indieweb.org/events/2026-05-13-hwc-nuremberg 2026-05-13].

Thanks to {{tilman}} for hosting us at the [https://d.th-nuernberg.de/en/ design faculty of Technische Hochschule Nürnberg Georg Simon Ohm].

See also the matching entry on the [https://homebrew-website.club/t/homebrew-website-club-10-juni-2026/ HWC Nuremberg Discourse].

== Notes ==

[[File:hwc-nuremberg-2026-06.jpg|thumb|alt=Selfies of the participants of HWC Nuremberg 2026/06 made with Tilmans photo app|Selfies of the participants of HWC Nuremberg 2026/06 made with Tilmans photo app]]

The meeting started with a discussion if (and how) we should make the next HWC event, which will be part of the Nürnberg Digital Festival, more visible. We settled on promoting it on social media ([[Mastodon]], [[LinkedIn]] and a local game development community [[Discord]]). Furthermore we had a look at other interesting events that are part of the festival, e.g. the Digital Women * Day. 

We also plan to have our regular HWC meeting the week after this special edition event, and we hope to have some retention of participants.

After finishing this administrative talk, we dove into some technical demos.

René showed his Coolify installation, which is an open-source [[Netlify]] clone that can be self-hosted. He installed it on his own server that is hosted locally in Nuremberg by Hetzner. René currently runs an instance of Strapi on it, but plans to move some further services there. He also talked about the WebExpo Prague that he attended recently, which had a really unstructured keynote by SwitchAngel (of Strudel and YouTube fame). Fortunately, this was compensated by a better talk my Mike Kus later on.

Max currently tries to re-use a website concept from 2013, where the site is split diagonally in two different parts (black and white) which can be switched by a mouse click. He was amazed that this old project could be imported in a modern [[Wordpress]] instance quite easily.

Tilman teamed up with Claude to get his website working on mobile again. He also fixed some bugs so that his RSS feed works again (yay!). There is also a new entry in his digital garden about the Platformer Game Jam that took part in May at the Technische Hochschule Georg Simon Ohm. It's amazing that the platformers that were created run flawlessly with audio and gamepad support in the browser, thanks to the Godot engine and WebAssembly.
Tilman also improved his website by implementing Plausible Analytics and (vibe-)coded a mobile camera web app which we used to take the event photo.

Nora asked if anyone has experience with Penpot as an open source alternative to Figma, and René gave the feedback that he had some issues with it, but she should try it out as in general it is quite usable.

In the end René also demoed the latest drafts for a new UI for his journaling tool. He was inspired by the AI analysis Dan Catt did with his journaling entries and decided to create a new UI design with Figma. After settling for a design of his choice, he requested some feedback of the participants.
The tool he wants to create should be deployed on his Coolify instance, but should also work as an app using Tauri.
