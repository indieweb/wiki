== Design in CSS ==
* James asked about link text wrapping on his homepage "Recent Essays" section https://jamesg.blog/
** multiple CSS grid and other options worked on:
*** Using text-overflow: ellipsis... to cut off long links along with a max-width
*** Making the middle container bigger using CSS grid `max-content`
*** Using subgrid and baseline alignment
*** Adding padding below li
*** grid columns can be in max-content or min-content but (apparently) not fit-content
*** adding a overflow: hidden + text-overflow: ellipsis + a :hover state with overflow: unset and max-width: unset
*** {{gRegor}} found this method using `padding-left: 1rem; text-indent: -1rem;` for the link lines that wrap: https://stackoverflow.com/a/480596
*** I also found that `text-indent` doesn't seem to work on a `::first-line` pseudo selector, I think because of this from [[MDN]] "The indentation set by the property occurs at the inline-start edge of the content box." https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Properties/text-indent
* "You have the markup that you have, and you're trying to accomplish a goal."
** Refactoring markup is a lot of work.
** You cook with the ingredients you have.
** "You might need one more div. Famous last words."
