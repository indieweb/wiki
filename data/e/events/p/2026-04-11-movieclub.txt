=== March ===
* https://artlung.com/surf-imc/
* Endless Summer
** Culturally powerful in California
** Al went to UCSD / Muir College - surfers went there, lived with 90s surf college, cliquey, child of priviledge
* Endless Summer - has problematic stereotypes
** Friend had an idea for a surf camp - only pros had video of their surfing - GoPro - capture action
** February - Sophia chose a movie Winged Migration

** Joe: 
** it's basically a 16mm home video of a road trip, very scrappy
** not distributed in a traditional way, was very much a cult hit
** doesn't show women, has problematic views of Africa, but also introduced many people to a worldwide vagabond surf culture for the first time
** even highlighted some local spots like The Wedge
** shows belly boards and bodysurfing
** does not reference the times of the 60's at all, it's purely about getting in the water

* Girls Can't Surf
** quite a contrast with Endless Summer because it tackled culture head on
** made us reflect back on the 90's in a way that we didn't appreciate (thought we were so evolved and not at all)
** powerful moment where a women's prize check went viral on Facebook for being half the size of the men
** women and people of color are way more popular in the water now
** surfing used to have really big issues with localism and violence
** still a monied sport, boards start at like $800, you need wetsuits, transportation, etc
** way more democratic these days, check out Dylan Graves, a creator that made a series for a shoe company, called Weird Waves about the lengths people will go to surf
** https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLEfn8qMSGQa8kVZtBtIWfH_3wU_iE7vJQ
** Joe watches everything he makes, the guy's still going strong despite no more Vans sponsorship
** Dylan loves highlighting diverse representation, like an episode about queer surfers
** "Waves are finite" - there's only so many good waves in spots and there's a heirarchy of access
** Now the crowds in places like Malibu are intense
** Contrast with the indieweb is that iwc is all about sharing, surf spots are semi-hidden and intentionally not geolocated (Al: cycling is like that too, especially unofficial trails, same in the UK)
** Surfing has really changed over the years since Endless Summer and the "unemployable vagabond bums" into the commercialization era highlighted in Girls Can't Surf
** Mike: a lot of similarity to On The Road
** Lots of post-WW2 prosperity inherent in hitchhiking and "dirtbagging" travel
** Trading horror stories about kids bumming around Baja to catch waves

The Surfer - with Nic Cage - deals with the poor culture and heirarchy, controlling the beach.

Great suggested movie pairing that's similar in spirit: Lords of Dogtown and Z Boys
* New skater bringing back old school styles with a beautiful movie set in Paris: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4q8wZ15ALz4

Joe on the explosion of gopro, youtube and Patreon: "it's possible to make Endless Summer all the time"
* They've even gone to the moon the last two weeks: https://gopro.com/en/us/news/gopro-cameras-aboard-nasa-artemis-ii-moon-mission?srsltid=AfmBOoo8DTnm8fQSUh11lqyXxbmau5EolyjPJO3c3_XrhL17ACYY5vci
