* Welcome to HWC!
* What we're doing on our websites
** Paulo has relaunched his website and is translating articles in his digital garden

* Idea: writing about our favourite writers
* We'll never run out of things to do on our websites!
* If we could only take four books on a trip, what would they be?
* Reading the same book twice: it's not the same book
* We are always getting new information -- tomorrow we will have new information that will help us understand what we read the day before.

* Building our sites to figure out what we want them to do, then rebuilding the website when we know what we want to build.

* Thomas: building his website for 25 years and is still not finished
** Transformations over the years: big shift in 2000-2001 to add a blog, starting with Blogger then his own CMS
** Used same design for a lot of the time; changed typefaces as screens got larger and resolutions got better
** Converted site from PHP 5 to 8 last year, and updated blog management tools in the process

* What was the biggest change in our websites since we started them?
** Making our sites a little bit more dynamic; more of a garden
** Had website serving only on non-standard ports because didn't want people to go there, like the law in some places that makes you put your address online; now site is more public, been more inspired to write things on mind without self-censoring.
** Changing from a single HTML page that was like a /now page to a fully-fledged website.
** Not being stuck to only one theme on our websites; bringing multiple themes together to build our website. Absorbing information from all sorts of places and from many subjects as inspiration.
*** Building digital garden with Obsidian locally, then had idea to make it public. Used Obsidian Publish.
** No pressure to update daily / several times per week; finding our own paces. Having sections for notes and the wiki. "Two worlds on the same web page."
