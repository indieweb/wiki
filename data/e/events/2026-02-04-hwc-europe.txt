=== Questions Discussed ===

* What do people do with webmentions? Do they have them? What service do they use? Do they export/back them up somewhere
** {{capjamesg}} loves webmentions and has accepted them for years. He keeps his private, and recieves them in his RSS Reader, that way he doesn't have to moderate them
** Think of them as a comments section or just a notification system
** You can render them, cache them, display them on your site once you decide on how to present them if you want to
** You don't have to do anything with them!
** Webmentions are mostly spam-free, but it's more of a when, not if situation
* The month's IndieWeb Carnival prompt: 'Intersecting Interests' could also be applicable to thinking through different points of view on a subject
* The constant push-pull between exploring new tools and finding concrete use-cases for them in life
** And getting too deep within something and it comes up at work and adds to your responsibilities
** If you stick it to the man and the man is yourself, what then? 'You don't tell me what to do, I tell me what to do!'
* Down with gray text on a white background or gray text on a black background
* How do you think about your projects and sites long term? What infastructure do they depend on, and what is their longevity
* Sites are inherently flexible: instant updates
* The nature of change on the web
** Website hosting as torrent file sharing
** If we want more people to participate in the web, we need to give them access to infastructure
** Paul Watson has a relevant post about this: https://www.lazaruscorporation.co.uk/blogs/artists-notebook/posts/where-do-i-see-the-indieweb-in-2030
** Apple used to offer a persona webspace as part of their user accounts. 
*** Related: iLife which came with a trial of iWork
* Apple Keynote vs Microsoft Powerpoint for creating presentations
** {{vanderwal}}'s stuff was used in a Steve Jobs' keynote ("he didn't respond which was a good response")
* File-sharing as a law of attraction model
* Scribd bought Slideshare and made a mess of things: where to host slides?
* The 'law of attraction' and 'day in the life' models recieving widespread use
* Wanting to be able to pike pieces of software but also customize them
** As a developer, perhaps rather than the first instinct being 'how can i build this' when not happy with a tool, could it be 'how could i modify this to suit'?
** Rather than seeing software as silos, how can they be maximally open
** Data is better open-shared than interfaces
** I want my feed reader to be my calendar and my search engine and own my data
*** Calendar subscriptions to ics files in an RSS Reader
** Progress in software shouldn't be new gimmicks, but rather how things intergrate and their trustworthiness
** A long-time principle should be portable, interoperable file formats and data presentations
** The internet depends on small projects often maintained by single people in their spare time
** There's great skill in being a developer who can pull multiple things together
** Could software be decentralized and still work, like Mastodon does?
*** ActivityPub proves there's much potential in this area
*** ATProtocol vs ActivityPub
