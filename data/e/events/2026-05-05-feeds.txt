== Notes ==
* Welcome to the Feeds pop up!
* You can see our notes from the IWC DUS Feeds session at [[2026/D%C3%BCsseldorf/Feeds]] Using [[FreshRSS]] as a back-end and [[NetNewsWire]]
* Social readers may have more features than simple RSS ones?
* As a publisher: tags have feeds, a series can have a feed
** Paul wants to offer different feeds to people
* How do we make feeds friendlier?
** Most recent Apple Podcasts still lets you paste in an arbitrary feed URL to subscribe; "Follow a Show by URL"; "if it's good enough for apple"
* [[SubToMe]] lets you create a follow button
** gRegor: I use SubToMe on https://gregorlove.com/follow/ so when you click one of the "follow" links there, it has a modal over the page with popular feed reader options. I believe it lets people customize the reader options or default to their preferred one
* [[Subscribe Openly]] lets you generate URLs to automatically follow a reader
** https://subscribeopenly.net/subscribe/?url=https://jamesg.blog/feeds/posts.xml
* How do we avoid using the term "[[RSS]]"?
* gRegor: I use "follow all posts", "follow notes," "follow articles," etc.
* Could an RSS reader let you subscribe to feeds without creating an account, and then ask you to confirm your account?
** One problem: what if someone doesn't create an account and doesn't realise they will lose their data?
* Preview could be for everyone, but adding a feed could require sign up
* Apple has iTunes extensions for RSS which enhances the experience in Apple Podcasts, but you can use regular XML if you want
* Does Apple's Podcast app directly retrieve feeds from the device?
* Create a feed for your website with an empty audio file so people could subscribe via Apple Podcasts? (might not be too useful, but would be fun!)
* Browsers used to show an icon when you went to a feed; there are extensions for this now.
* Was there something like mailto: for RSS?
** Browsers show dialog if you don't have a mailto set up
* XSLT makes a feed less intimidating; you can add styles instead of showing plain XML in a browser
* Could there be a feed:// handler?
* https://codeberg.org/emersion/gamja (client) https://soju.im (bouncer) for IRC
** offered to open all IRC links through gamja in the browser
* If you POSSE your content to Mastodon, etc, people could follow you there
* Mike has a Mastodon account that publishes links to his website
* James uses Brid.gy Fed to announce new articles with the title and link
* How can we introduce more people to the world of web readers?
* Having people following your feed directly gives you control rather than being disintermediated by sharing links on a platform
* Having separate feeds for links and content
* All the feed formats are fragmented: RSS, Atom, h-feed
* Do people have preferences of a format a publisher?
** Daniel prefers Atom because of all the attributes
** James prefers h-feed because it's on the web
** gRegor -- publishes h-feed, uses [[Granary]] to convert to Atom
*** gregorlove.com/notes/feed.atom redirects to Atom, ending up with a Granary URL
*** It is advisable to do a temporary redirect so that you can change your feed URL at any point
** Tantek has an Atom file and has had no reports of issues
*** Tantek tries to put as much as possible on his h-feed on his home page
*** https://micro.blog/t shows his top-level blog posts on tantek.com, via his Atom feed
*** Atom feed doesn't include responses, only original posts
* RSS is the lowest common denonimator
* You could offer every feed but that is a lot of overhead
* Is there a reason to have RSS and Atom?
** You should only really need one.
** Main thing is someone can subscribe and in some format
* [[WebSub]] https://www.w3.org/TR/websub/
* [[Artemis]] lets you follow ActivityPub accounts but doesn't add replies to users' feeds, except for replies published by the author to their own posts (which would imply the user is creating a thread)
* Firehoses as a pattern -- some feeds publish really high volumes of information by design, like news feeds or news aggregator feeds.
* Putting things in separate lists / folders helps to ensure things don't take up too much space
** https://feed.city supports this
** Artemis supports folders too
* There are lots of readers out there -- it's worth checking what options are out there!
* The "inbox zero" phenomena -- how do you achieve inbox zero?
* https://www.currentreader.app/ follows the concept of having a "river"
** new feed reader with a "river" metaphor https://www.terrygodier.com/current
** “Reading should feel like floating, not drowning.”
** https://dynamicskillset.github.io/stream/ open source "current"-like feed reader-
** Curious: "Stream is a frontend only."
* Is there a way to promote posts by people who haven't written in a while?
* Is there a MIME type for [[JSON Feed]]?
** https://www.jsonfeed.org/version/1.1/ <code>application/feed+json</code>
** Look for jsonfeed.org in version key to see if it is a valid JSON Feed
* Daniel: wish more people would support [[WebSub]]
** Polling is not efficient
* Is there a list of readers that support WebSub?
** [[WebSub]] You need to send requests to a hub if WebSub isn't automatically integrated
** There should be visibility to show if a request failed
* Federating posts to other platforms
** You might not syndicate everything / people might not syndicate everything
** Most destinations don't support the richness of content that you have on your website; text notes will go everywhere, but HTML doesn't carry over. The same is true of RSS / web readers.
** People see a lower fidelity version of the content than is what on your website.
** Reason to avoid "follow me on [platform]" wording because people will not see everything, but you can see "here are other places I am".
** Photos may appear at bottom of post on Mastodon, etc. even if the photo is inline in context on your website
* https://paultibbetts.uk/follow/
* There is no easy way to see how a page is rendered in multiple web readers like Feedly, etc.
** Al mentioned that readers didn't represent a photo article that he wrote in the same way
* also some destinations re-order content e.g. federating puts a photo at the end of anote:
** https://cosocial.ca/@evan/116521276502446266
** preview on Monocle: https://monocle.p3k.io/preview?url=https://tantek.com/2026/124/t1/citrus-club-closed 
** As a web author you may be particular about how you want things to be displayed; external destinations may not align with your editorial preferences.
* Would people want things in their reader other than posts, like their calendar?
** You could use RSS with Thunderbird: which puts your reader and calendar and inbox in close proximity: https://blog.thunderbird.net/2022/05/thunderbird-rss-feeds-guide-favorite-content-to-the-inbox/
* People used to put event posts / RSVP posts in their feeds
** A good reader could pull those into a calendar view
** Emails often let you easily add things to a calendar, like when you open a calendar invite in Gmail
* A reader doesn't need to write to your system calendar
** Could offer iCS for both individual events and full calendars
** Artemis supported iCS subscription; this is no longer supported
* [[Together]] (https://alltogethernow.io/) pulled out all photos from posts and created a gallery
* Anything time-series could be a feed
* https://www.scottishtechnology.club/
* It would be cool if you could follow concert announcements via RSS
* James' feature request for [[FeedCity]]: it would be great to be able to have multiple people add to a collective feed like https://feed.city/c/dan/l/IWC+DUS+2026.
* FeedCity coming soon: subscribe to OPML files
* https://ooh.directory/blogs/arts/ has OPML files for every tag
* https://feedland.org/ has some OPML-based features too
* Tantek: if encounter response posts, thread with original post
** Artemis has https://jamesg.blog/2026/04/01/artemis-nesting-social-posts, but might not work for replies right now
** https://jamesg.blog/2026/02/22/grouping-threaded-posts-in-artemis groups by author threads
** Feed readers may need a control that says don't show replies, or only show original content, or only show specific types of content per feed or per person (only show long-form posts, etc.).
* There are lots of features in feed formats that are not utilised in readers
