=== Manny ===
* Search result shows on the same page
* Top header looks a bit busy, want to move search to its own page
* Feel tired about site's colour theme, don't want to see blue because it is associated with work software
** Want to use a new colour theme, called Warm Burnout: https://github.com/felipefdl/warm-burnout
** WCAG compliant, accessibility and AAA colour contrast is important
** CSS filter on all non-image elements, buttons, icons, and even text (to exclude) - and just change the hue on everything else
** It's one layer on top of the whole site without changing all the independent variables
** https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Selectors/:not
** https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Values/filter-function/hue-rotate

*** Can be an alternative-unconvential but quick way to set dark mode on a site

=== Q: IndieAuth login on sites? ===
** Pablo has one, it will show a greeting "Hi, <yourdomain>!"
** Gregor thought of adding it, has email login? 
** Angelo's site shows your name on the page if logged in, but to access protected content he has to add you to the audience property for that particular page
*** Can make a "click-baity" image (for fun) that shows a blurred image of a box, and entice people to login with IndieAuth to unblur
** Sometimes, Angelo's site will redirect to GitHub login alternative instead of your IndieAuth provider
* IndieAuth scopes: 
** profile: returns name, photo, url 
** email: returns email address
** No custom login page though
* Codeberg doesn't offer as tightly scoped tokens as GitHub
* https://getindiekit.com/specifications#indieauth
*https://indieauth.spec.indieweb.org/#access-token-verification
* Requesting a token when logging in means access can: 
** State and code verifier should be dropped after authentication
** Show email, name, and email with user option to remove. keep rest of indieAuth data private for safety
* Private webmentions, [[Private-Webmention]] as a mean of notifications?
