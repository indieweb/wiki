=== De-Googling ===
* Sophia recently switched calendars Google > Proton
** General consensus is that Gmail has better phishing / spam filters than Proton as there's targeted phishing spam that gets through in Proton sometimes
** Did not like all the AI tools being forced onto user
* Mike feels stuck with Gmail, no good macOS client, but has good amount of storage, is reliable, and "good" spam filter
* David suggests changing one product/tool at a time, not a whole platform in one go - too much
** e.g. Wordpress > ClassicPress
* Q: what are some email hosting platform suggestions outside the common Gmail, iCloud, Proton?
* Fastmail?
* For those that are not as well informed about phishing and dangers of links etc - it makes Apple's ecosystem a bit safer as it's more locked down
** But Android is also becoming locked down, https://keepandroidopen.org/, which upsets those who want the freedom

* How should we organize posts? 
** Balance between multiple posts vs one comphrensive/long post
** Sophia aims to do 10 of one category, for example: https://www.fractalkitty.com/tag/inquiries/
or doing a set of 52 (one a week)
** Having a collection of similar posts is appealing, using a theme/link to tie content together
* Self hosting blogs is a bit like having a child/baby! Need to fix things in the middle of the night when something goes down
* Q: How do you present link pages?
** Tools, resources, people, elsewhere
*# Most people have just a list of links, Mike has a template he uses with WP to generate links
** XFN, categorize links (friendship, location met, professional relation) https://gmpg.org/xfn/
** A bit risky for social engineering phishing hacks if listing this information
* Internet in a box, https://internet-in-a-box.org/
** When Internet access is down, it can serve as a way to access big databases/common sites
** A good site to have is Wikipedia, or archive.org
** Depends on how much space available

==== Dropbox Woes ====
* Lost data there before by having music stored
* Constant nagging to upgrade, and actively not wanting you to do full backup locally
* Local backup is important as the cloud is just someone else's computer!
** Files get deleted when it can't find the file (if external drive fails to connect), which results in data loss
