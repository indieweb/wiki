    '''<dfn>ICANN</dfn>''' is a global organization that is responsible for running the [[personal-domain|Domain Name]] Service root name servers and is also responsible for the assignment of IPv4 and IPv6 addresses.

Under ICANN, IANA is the central authority responsible for mapping IP addresses to domain names. ICANN also controls the creation of [[top-level_domain|TLDs]], the management of which ICANN typically delegates to outside organizations. Alternatives to ICANN's Domain Name root are discouraged by the Internet Architecture Board<ref>https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc2826</ref>, as the World Wide Web's domain system was designed to be centralized. 

ICANN is responsible for verifying e-mail addresses when domain contacts change, which can be a problem as documented in https://levels.io/icann-mafia/.
