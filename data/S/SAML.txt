'''<dfn>SAML</dfn>''' is an older set of identity standards often used by enterprises and governments for single-sign-on that has a trusted certificate single point of vulnerability, exploited used in in December 2020 to sign-into numerous high value US government at other accounts, in contrast to [[IndieAuth]], which by its distributed nature has no single point of certificate vulnerability.
