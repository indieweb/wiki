A '''<dfn>domain registrar</dfn>''' is an organization that will register [[domain names]] for your [[personal domain]] for an annual fee. A domain can be pointed at a [[site|website]] and/or as a domain-based handle as in [[ATProto]]. 

== Considerations ==
* '''Configurability''' - You should be able to set up all record types ([[DNS#A_record|A]], [[CNAME]], [[MX]], [[DNS#TXT Record|TXT]]) for as many [[subdomains]] as you'd like/need.
* '''[[Email]] forwarding''' - does the registrar offer free email forwarding? This is useful if you want an @yourdomain.com email address that forwards to an existing account.  
* '''Auto-renew''' - it's easy to accidentally let a domain name registration expire; some registrars let you set up email reminders or an automatic renewal to prevent this from happening. 
* '''Domain [[privacy|Privacy]]''' - Note that most domain name registrars will make your personal information (name, mailing address, phone number and email address) publicly available via whois lookups. Some registrars offer domain privacy options, so that instead of your personal details the registrar's details will be in the whois directory. Only use domain privacy if you fully trust the provider of the service -- disputes about domain name administration or transfers may get tricky if you are not listed as the legal owner of the domain.
* '''Bundled [[hosting]]'''? Shop around for the best registrar and best host for your needs. They do not need to be the same.

==== Cloudflare ====
* https://domains.cloudflare.com/
* Used and recommended by [[User:Nickgray.net|Nickgray.net]]
* A bit complicated UI, but excellent pricing

==== DNSimple ====
{{main|DNSimple}}
* https://dnsimple.com
* Used by {{jacky}}
** {{jacky}} 30 Nov 2019: Excellent UX, [[2FA]] support

==== Freenom ====
* [https://freenom.com Freenom], a provider of many [[free-domain-names|free domain names]]
* Used for some ancillary sites by {{beesbuzz.biz}}
* Allows IDN registrations (including emoji)
* Domains are private by default
* You can also opt to pay for a domain if you want an ownership guarantee
* Downside: used by a lot of spammers; don't use a free domain for your email address if you want people to actually read it
