A '''<dfn>directory</dfn>''' on the web lists websites or pages typically grouped by topic or areas of interest, sometimes using categories or [[tags]], often useful for finding niche communities and [[discovery|discovering]] new websites. (Also known as a web directory.)

'''See [[#Examples_In_The_Wild|Examples in the Wild]]'''

== Why ==

[[Silo|Silos]] often provide [[recommendation engine|recommendation engines]] or ideas about [[who to follow]] to give their users content to [[readers|read]]. As IndieWeb adherents begin to own their own data on their own websites, they'll want services similar to these whether they are centralized or decentralized.

== Examples In The Wild ==
{{main|indieweb directory}}
* '''[https://bukmark.club/ Bukmark Club]''' is a collection of websites that have a curated list of bookmarks and/or links to other websites.

* '''[https://melonland.net/surf-club MelonLand Surf Club]''' is a directory of personal sites

* '''[https://blogofthe.day Blog of the .Day]''' features a random indieweb blog each day. It features an [[RSS]] feed one can subscribe to. Instructions for including a blog appear on the site. Created by {{capjamesg}}, now stewarded by {{artlung}}.

* '''[https://blogsurf.io/ Blog Surf]''' was a directory both of sites and of blog posts. As of 2020-02-27, it indexed [https://blogsurf.io/blogs 752 blogs]. These blogs were categorised by tags within the directory. The directory also kept a list of posts made by these blogs and was able to show either the latest posts of the whole collection, or on a per-tag basis. As of 2025-12-23, the domain has been bought by another party.

* '''[https://nownownow.com/ Now Now Now]''' is directory built by Derek Sivers. See also: [[now]]

* '''[https://ooh.directory ooh directory]''' is a directory of blogs personally curated by [https://www.gyford.com/ Phil Gyford]. There is a list of [https://ooh.directory/about/faq/ criteria for inclusion]. The main criteria is the inclusion of an [[Atom]] or [[RSS]] feed, be updated in the last few months, self-submit. It also includes a , be in English, among other criteria. It includes a directory by category, a [[https://ooh.directory/random/ random blog page]], a list of updated list of [[https://ooh.directory/updated/ recently updated blogs]] with their most recent post. See [[ooh.directory]]

* [[OwnCast]] runs a directory of '''[https://directory.owncast.online OwnCast instances]'''. You can opt your instance in to publishing to the directory, and your instance will appear at the top when it's actively streaming.

* '''[https://personalsit.es/ Personal sites are awesome]''' is a website built by [https://andy-bell.design/notes/104/ Andy Bell] using a [[Github]] repo so we can all discover each others websites. All the links are by folks that want to share their site with the world.

* '''[https://wildwild.directory The Wild Wild Web]''' is a directory of cool websites, organized and curated by humans. It's a modern take on Yahoo Directory, focusing on non-commercial sites that have something to say, not something to sell.

* '''[http://tilde.club/~pfhawkins/othertildes.html A list of tilde.club servers]''' with sites for a variety of people and users and based on the original [[tilde.club]].

* '''[https://www.awwwards.com/ Awwwards]''' has a site of the day. People can nominate, vote, and browse a directory of sites.

* '''[https://blogblog.es/ ¡Blog!¡Blog!]''' is a blog directory that lists websites whose authors write in Spanish.

* '''[[unmung]]'s Mastoview''' is very similar to a directory with a randomizer built in.

== Past Examples ==
* [[Indieweb.xyz]] was a syndication service / directory organized into various subs, similar to Reddit, created by [http://kickscondor.com Kicks Condor]. All interaction with the site, such as linking and upvoting, was performed by Webmention. Discussion happens on blog posts themselves, as per Indieweb convention.

* [https://colinwalker.blog/ Colin Walker] had a [https://colinwalker.blog/directory/ personal directory] (404 as of 2024-11-16) comprised of websites that have sent his site [[Webmention|webmentions]].

* [https://the.dailywebthing.com/ the dailywebthing linkport] is a hybrid directory/linkblog operating from 2004-2023, and its sister site, [https://pointers.dailywebthing.com/ dailywebthing daily pointers], since 2000, Both created by [https://jenett.org/ Joe Jenett]

* "IndieWeb Directory on Glitch" (<nowiki>indieweb-directory.glitch.me</nowiki>) was a list of people with personal websites on the IndieWeb who have signed-in with [[IndieAuth]] and was hosted on [[Glitch]]. The list of people that have saved their sites did not seem to have any particular order or categorization.

* [https://districts.neocities.org/ Neocities Districts] was "a site directory created on Neocities for Neocities". A place to discover other people who have been creating (mostly oldschool) web pages about their interests on [[Neocities]]. As of 2025-12-23, the directory has been discontinued.

* [http://community.bergbuilds.domains/ Berg Builds Community] was a collection of Domain of One's Own projects at Muhlenberg College.

* [https://refined.blog/ Refined Blog] was a list of personal software blogs collected and organized from various sources with [[Hacker News]] scores calculated/displayed as an indicator of value to the [[collection]]. Included links to feeds as well as the ability to generate an [[OPML]] of the list.

* [https://nodes.indieseek.xyz/ Hyperlink Node Directory] (currently generating PHP errors as of 2024-11-16) was a project by [https://ramblinggit.com Brad Enslen] comprised of a collection of web links. It could be considered a directory of directories, and it could be used for [[discovery]] of new websites and for surfing the web.
