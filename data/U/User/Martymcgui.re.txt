=== Silo Posting ===

While I try to treat [[silos]] as purely places where I publish syndication, there are still some things I will post only to silos.

* Twitter
** Some likes, retweets, and even quick replies that I don't consider "long-term relevant" enough to keep on my site.
* Facebook
** I typically do not post FB reactions such as likes, loves, "haha", etc. to my site. In general I am trying to stop using Facebook reactions (because [https://motherboard.vice.com/en_us/article/how-our-likes-helped-trump-win dang] and [http://brucesterling.tumblr.com/post/159412046858/targeting-options-for-facebook-advertisers gosh]).
** Responses to prompts from FB like encouragement to send birthday posts. I am also using these less and less.
** Comments to other people's FB posts. Unless I started the thread, I generally don't think it's appropriate to copy it to my site.

== What Works ==

* Identity h-card and [[RelMeAuth]]
* [[micropub]] using a custom [[Flask]] endpoint, with [[indieauth.com]] for [[IndieAuth]] authorization.
** File uploads go to a separate [https://www.w3.org/TR/micropub/#media-endpoint media endpoint], running on [https://github.com/martymcguire/spano Spano].
** I typically post using [[Quill]], both from my desktop browser and via a homescreen bookmark on Android.
** Audio content such as podcasts are posted using [[Screech]]
* Content is served as a [[static-site]] compiled using [[Jekyll]]
** [[permalinks]] are <code>/YYYY/mm/dd/HHMMSS/</code> (Eastern Time)
* [[short-domains|short domain]] mmg.re
** [[permashortlinks]] using [[newbase60]] to convert permalink paths into 6 characters (3 for days-since-epoch, 3 for seconds-since midnight)
** Custom Jekyll plugin makes these available to posts when rendering
** Custom Flask endpoint resolves these when visiting mmg.re. Unparseable requests are redirected as-is.
* Content
