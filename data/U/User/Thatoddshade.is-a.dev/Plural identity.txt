'''<dfn>Plurality</dfn>''', also known as <i>multiplicity</i>, is the concept of having multiple identities co-exist inside a single brain, while still retaining the ability to distinguish themselves, be inactive and communicate with each other.

People who identify as plural typically call their whole self as a ''system'' or a ''collective'', where each ‘personality’ is described as a ''member'' or an ''alter''. Non-plural people can be qualified as ''singlets''.

Not all members are always active—they always are there, but not necessarily ‘awake’. Members who have been inactive for a while may be said <dfn>dormant</dfn>.

<dfn>Fronting</dfn> refers to the control of the system’s body. There is always one ‘front’, which may be accompanied by other members, who do the act of ''co-fronting''.

Among the members of a system, there may be one serving the role of the ''host'', which can mean fronting the most regularly, appearing before every other member and other things.

Plurality is agreed to be often related to dissociative disorders, usually <abbr>DID</abbr> (Dissociative Identity Disorder), but the existence systems which are not caused by a disorder, ''endogenic systems'', remains debatable.
