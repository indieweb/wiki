'''<dfn>[https://www.unicode.org/ Unicode]</dfn>''' is a standard for encoding characters (letters, numbers, etc) in computer data.

== Tools ==

* [http://www.fileformat.info/info/unicode/char/search.htm Unicode Character Search] on [http://www.fileformat.info/ FireFormat.info]
** Search by glyph, number, or (partial) name.
** Every character has its own information [[permalink]].
* [https://r12a.github.io/applist#unicodestuff Unicode web apps] by [https://r12a.github.io/ Richard Ishida]
** [https://r12a.github.io/uniview/ UniView] for browsing the entirety of Unicode.
** [https://r12a.github.io/app-conversion/ Unicode code converter] for getting HTML entities and percent encodings of a string (amongst others).
* [http://qaz.wtf/u/ Unicode Toys] by Elijah Griffin
** for things like <abbr title="braille">⠃⠗⠁⠊⠇⠇⠑</abbr>, <abbr title="upside down">ndsıpǝ poʍu</abbr>, and <abbr title="mirrored">bɘᴙoᴙᴙim</abbr> text.

=== Copy-paste characters ===

* [https://www.copypastecharacter.com/ CopyPasteCharacter.com]
* [http://unicodefractions.com/ Unicode (Vulgar) Fractions]
** You can use the [http://qaz.wtf/u/fraction.cgi Unicode Fraction Maker] from the Unicode Toys to generate other fractions.
