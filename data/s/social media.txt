'''<dfn>social media</dfn>''' is the marketing term for [[silos]] and silo [[posts]], ironically more [http://powazek.com/posts/3368 anti-social] than ''social'', and [http://www.stephenfry.com/2016/02/peedinthepool/ encouraging of extreme opposition] rather than ''medium'' perspectives, typically with [[like]]/[[repost]] counts [[UI]]s that incentivize inflammatory posts rather than considered thoughts &amp; dialog.

== Criticism ==
=== Harmful To Democracy ===
* 2016-11-15 (updated 2018-01-05) Vox: [https://www.vox.com/policy-and-politics/2016/11/15/13593670/donald-trump-social-media-culture-politics Why social media is terrible for multiethnic democracies]
* 2018-08-28 [https://unherd.com/2018/08/social-media-makes-fascists-us/  How social media makes fascists of us all]
* 2018-12-17 NYTimes: [https://www.nytimes.com/2018/12/17/us/politics/russia-2016-influence-campaign.html Russian 2016 Influence Operation Targeted African-Americans on Social Media]<blockquote>The Russian influence campaign on social media in the 2016 election made an extraordinary effort to target African-Americans, used an array of tactics to try to suppress turnout among Democratic voters and unleashed a blizzard of activity on Instagram that rivaled or exceeded its posts on Facebook</blockquote>
* 2019-01-22 Vox: [https://www.vox.com/policy-and-politics/2019/1/22/18177076/social-media-facebook-far-right-authoritarian-populism Social media is rotting democracy from within] / How social platforms enable far-right politicians’ campaigns to undermine democracy. <blockquote><p>At the inauguration of Brazil’s new far-right president, Jair Bolsonaro, in early January, a crowd of his supporters began a surprising chant. They weren’t cheering for Bolsonaro or his running mate or their party; instead, they were reciting the names of social media platforms.</p><p>“Facebook, Facebook, Facebook!” the crowd yelled. “WhatsApp, WhatsApp, WhatsApp!”</p></blockquote>

=== Harms Productivity ===
* ~2015(?) [http://www.nealstephenson.com/social-media.html Neal Stephenson - Social Media] <blockquote>Though I never became a heavy poster on social media, I did reach a point last year when I was checking Twitter and my personal Facebook every few minutes. When I found myself manually refreshing the Facebook window in the hopes of dredging up posts I hadn't seen yet, I decided it was time to think a little harder about the effect that this was having on my ability to get things done.</blockquote>

=== May Harm Career ===
* 2016-11-19 NYT: [http://www.nytimes.com/2016/11/20/jobs/quit-social-media-your-career-may-depend-on-it.html Quit Social Media. Your Career May Depend on It.]
** Counterpoints: 2016-12-03 NYT: [http://www.nytimes.com/2016/12/03/jobs/dont-quit-social-media-put-it-to-work-for-your-career-instead.html Don’t Quit Social Media. Put It to Work for Your Career Instead.]
