'''<dfn>Small Web</dfn>''' is a phrase used in a [https://www.sciencemag.org/news/1999/09/its-small-web-after-all 1999 article], a [https://smallweb.net/ hosting service], two [https://thesmallweb.co/ consulting] [http://www.thesmallwebpeople.co.uk/ services], more recently a [https://neustadt.fr/essays/the-small-web/ May 2020 article], and repurposed in a [https://ar.al/2020/08/07/what-is-the-small-web/ Aug 2020 blog post] to describe (without citing) a subset of IndieWeb [[principles]], yet claiming to be exceptionally different, comparing aspirational ideals to practical IndieWeb solutions.

== Criticism of Aug 2020 repurposing ==

=== Nirvana fallacy ===
The 2020 August repurposing of "small web" and [https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=24269830 subsequent rhetoric] describes what the "small web" '''aspires to be''' in comparison to a narrowly-framed view of what the IndieWeb '''actually already is today in practice''', and thus is an example of [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nirvana_fallacy the Nirvana fallacy].

=== Digital greenwashing ===
From https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=24269071: <blockquote><p>Appropriate all the good values and ideas from https://indieweb.org/, re-heat it add some feel-good word salad and we have this "Small Web".</p><p>Yes, by all means let's get back to a more decentralized and distributed web. But providing a service where you are still hosting your site on someone else's servers and on someone else's domain and calling for values is just digital green-washing.</p></blockquote>
