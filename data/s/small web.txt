'''<dfn>Small Web</dfn>''' is a phrase used in a [https://www.sciencemag.org/news/1999/09/its-small-web-after-all 1999 article], a [https://smallweb.net/ hosting service], two [https://thesmallweb.co/ consulting] [http://www.thesmallwebpeople.co.uk/ services], a [https://neustadt.fr/essays/the-small-web/ May 2020 article], a [https://ar.al/2020/08/07/what-is-the-small-web/ Aug 2020 blog post] to aspirationally describe (without citing) a subset of IndieWeb [[principles]], and most recently a [https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=25251743 HackerNews summary] of [[Gemini]].

== Articles ==
* 1999-09-09: Science Magazine: [https://www.sciencemag.org/news/1999/09/its-small-web-after-all It's a Small Web After All]
* 2020-05-25: [https://neustadt.fr/essays/the-small-web/ Rediscovering the Small Web], written by Parimal Satyal, is a post extolling the virtues of handmade HTML web pages <blockquote>This essay is my attempt to show you what the small and independent web can look like, why it’s different from the the sites that dominate web traffic today, why it's worth exploring and how easy it is for anyone to be a part of it.</blockquote>
** See also: [https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=23326329 HN suggestions for sites that fit Parimal Satyal's definition of Small Web]
* 2020-08-07: [https://ar.al/2020/08/07/what-is-the-small-web/ What is the Small Web?]

== Criticism of Aug 2020 repurposing ==

=== Nirvana fallacy ===
The 2020 August repurposing of "small web" and [https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=24269830 subsequent rhetoric] describes what the "small web" '''aspires to be''' in comparison to a narrowly-framed view of what the IndieWeb '''actually already is today in practice''', and thus is an example of [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nirvana_fallacy the Nirvana fallacy].

=== Digital greenwashing ===
From https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=24269071: <blockquote><p>Appropriate all the good values and ideas from https://indieweb.org/, re-heat it add some feel-good word salad and we have this "Small Web".</p><p>Yes, by all means let's get back to a more decentralized and distributed web. But providing a service where you are still hosting your site on someone else's servers and on someone else's domain and calling for values is just '''digital green-washing'''.</p></blockquote>'''Emphasis''' added.
