A '''<dfn>zine</dfn>''' is a paper self-publication, often about non-mainstream topics, sometimes described as the precursor of personal blogs and IndieWeb.

== Resources & Guides ==
* [https://nicezines.skynash.co.uk/ Nice Zines] - references and guides
* [https://nashhigh.itch.io/zinearranger Zine Arranger] - tool to convert a multi-page PDF for printing and folding
* [https://spectrolite.app/ Spectrolite] - Mac program for arranging zines
* [https://yamino.itch.io/beginners-guide-to-digital-zines Beginner's Guide to Digital Zines]
* [https://wizardzines.com/ Wizard Zines] - A reputable programming educational zine project by Julia Evans.

== Digital Zines ==

Some projects publish zines in a digital format. The classification as zine then comes from the often collaborative creation process and following the cut and paste designs of many paper zines.

* [https://yesterweb.org/zine/ The Yesterweb Zine] filled with ideas, creations, and thoughts about the internet. As of 2022-02-27 there are three issues.
