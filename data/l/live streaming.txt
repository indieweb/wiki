<dfn>'''live streaming'''</dfn> is the practice of [[streaming]] media (mostly [[audio]] or [[video]]) in realtime from a camera and/or microphone, rather than a recording.
* See '''[[streaming]]''' for examples of viewing or listening to pre-recorded media.

== IndieWeb Examples ==
* [[2020/Austin|IndieWebCamp Austin 2020]] was livestreamed to indieweb.live
* [[User:WaterPigs.co.uk|Barnaby Walters]] posts audio live streams on his website since ???, as demoed at [[2016/Nuremberg/Demos#Barnaby_Walters|IndieWebCamp Nuremberg 2016]].
* {{TheGillies}} has streamed a feed of his living room at http://static.tyler.cat/live
* [https://kmikeym.com/ kmikeym] occasionally streams while working, and [https://news.kmikeym.com/k5m-con-2020/ streamed a conference in 2020], all streamed to https://live.kmikeym.com
* The [https://events.indieweb.org/2020/06/microformats-server-migration-livestream-dvH4cGJmru19 Microformats server migration work session] was livestreamed to indieweb.live
* {{chrisaldrich}} created the first live stream from his website on 2020-07-23 using [[Hapity]] and their plugin in combination with his [[WordPress]] site. https://boffosocko.com/2020/07/23/livestreaming-directly-to-my-website/
* Matt Steele 2020-08-12 [https://steele.blue/indieweb-wedding-livestream/ Indieweb Livestreaming your Wedding with Owncast]. Description of using [[OwnCast]] with [[POSSE]] to [[Zoom]].

== Silo Examples ==
* [[Twitch]] live streams have been used for IndieWebCamp intro talks, introductions, and create day demos.
* [[YouTube]] has a live streaming feature called "YouTube Live". It has been used to stream IndieWebCamps
* [[Facebook]] Live
* [[Instagram]] Live (AKA IG live)
* [[Periscope]]
* [[Mixer]]
