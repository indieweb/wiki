'''<dfn>[https://web.archive.org/web/20130401084116/http://blog.glow.io/ Glow]</dfn>''' was a project that supported integrations with Twitter and Facebook and a handful of social web standards, and [[former projects|was abandoned]], site last archived in 2013. 

Its <kbd>glow.io</kbd> domain appears to have been sold to an [[LLM|AI]] startup as of 2026.

Standards that were supported: [[Activity Streams]], [[Salmon]], [[PubSubHubbub]], and [[Portable Contacts]] per [https://web.archive.org/web/20130401084116/http://blog.glow.io/post/5898242839/what-is-glow 2011-05-27 post].

Glow was currently limited to glow.io subdomains if you want to try it out. At some point it was planned to be opened it up to any host/domain.

Unclear if Glow source was ever available anywhere or if it was only a service you could use on a subdomain.

Folks that were using it on their own site:
* [[User:DBounds|Darren Bounds]], author of glow, profile [https://web.archive.org/web/20110701115154/http://dbounds.glow.io/profile dbounds.glow.io/profile] (Internet Archive link) was last updated in 2011.

Other instances / earlier work:
* <span id="ooava"><nowiki>ooava.com</nowiki></span> (domain abandoned) was a personal project social node for publishing (photos, videos, status updates) and conversation on the web and Android. ooava was a personal site that evolved into the glow project.
