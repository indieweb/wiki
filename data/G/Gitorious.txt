'''<dfn>Gitorious</dfn>''' (<nowiki>http://getgitorious.com</nowiki>) was an open source [[git]] hosting and collaboration software which was later acquired by [[Gitlab]]. Development seems to have stopped with the last release in December 2014:
* {{citation
| title = Announcing Gitorious 3.2
| url = https://blog.gitorious.org/2014/12/04/announcing-gitorious-3-2/
| author = Gitorious Blog
| published = 2014-12-04
| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20141230022141/http://blog.gitorious.org/2014/12/04/announcing-gitorious-3-2/
}}
* {{citation
| title = GitLab acquires Gitorious to bolster its on premises code collaboration platform
| url = https://about.gitlab.com/blog/2015/03/03/gitlab-acquires-gitorious/
| author = Sytse Sijbrandij
| published = 2015-03-03
| archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20221227230745/https://about.gitlab.com/blog/2015/03/03/gitlab-acquires-gitorious/
}}

Gitorious.org, a hosted service based on the software, closed in May 2015, and users were encouraged to migrate the repositories to [[GitLab]]:
* {{citation
| title = Gitorious.org is dead, long live gitorious.org
| url = https://blog.gitorious.org/2015/04/15/gitorious-org-is-dead-long-live-gitorious-org/
| author = Gitorious Blog
| published = 2015-04-15
| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20160122051635/http://blog.gitorious.org/2015/04/15/gitorious-org-is-dead-long-live-gitorious-org/
}}

When it shut down, [https://twitter.com/textfiles/status/588740015166861312 Archive Team took over] the gitorious.org domain and continues to host the public content in read-only form. 
* 2023-02-09: The [[https]] certificate expired 2019-03-28 so <nowiki>https://gitorious.org/</nowiki> shows a security warning, but can still be browsed if you accept the warning and proceed.
