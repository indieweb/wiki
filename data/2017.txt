'''<dfn>IndieWeb Summit 2017</dfn>''' was 2017-06-24 through 2017-06-25 in Portland, Oregon; the seventh annual gathering for independent web [[creators]] of all kinds, from graphic artists, to designers, [[UX]] engineers, coders, hackers, to share ideas, actively work on [[selfdogfood|creating for their own]] personal websites, and [[build]] upon each others creations.

= IndieWeb Summit =
One of several [[IndieWebCamp]] [[events]] in [[Planning|2017]].

<div class="p-description description">'''Own your data.'''

We should all own the content we're creating, rather than just posting to third-party content silos. Publish on your own domain, and syndicate out to silos. 

Join us for two days of brainstorming &amp; creating at <span class="p-name summary">IndieWeb Summit</span> and be a part of advancing &amp; growing the indie web.</div>

=== Remote Participation ===
IndieWebCamp has a strong tradition of remote participation, typically using IRC, Google Hangouts and Etherpad during as well as after the event. 
* '''Add yourself to: [[2017/Guest_List#Remote_Participants|Remote Participants Guest List]]''' to indicate interest in remote participation!

This year, we'll be using Mozilla's system for remote participation. We will broadcast the main room on our [https://www.youtube.com/indiewebcamp YouTube channel], and if you would like to join the discussions you will be able to call in from a computer. You can download [https://v.mozilla.com/download.html?lang=en Vidyo Desktop] ahead of the event to speed up joining the call once we're live.

Note that Portland is on <abbr title="Pacific Daylight Time">PDT</abbr>, UTC-7.

If you can't get to the event, consider remote participating or finding/running an IndieWebCamp remote party near you.

=== Travel and Lodging ===
==== Airport ====
‚úàÔ∏è '''PDX Airport.''' If you are flying to Portland, Oregon, PDX is the airport you want.

==== Public Transit ====
üöà From the PDX airport, the MAX will drop you off downtown a few blocks from the venue. It costs $2.50 for a ticket. [https://goo.gl/maps/F3Ryv9SZDZ42 Public Transit directions]

==== Lyft or Uber ====
üöô You can also take Lyft or Uber from the airport, they will pick you up in the lower level, in a section named "Transportation Network Companies." The cost will be about $18-22 for UberX. More info on the [https://newsroom.uber.com/us-oregon/uberx-is-taking-off-at-pdx/ Uber blog].

==== Taxi ====
üöï If you want to take a local cab, Aaron recommends calling Radio Cab (503-227-1212) when you are just outside security, and they will meet you in the lower level for pickup. Alternately, there is usually a line of cabs ready to take people in the lower level.
