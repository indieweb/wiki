'''<dfn>IndieWebCamp Nuremberg 2026</dfn>''', the '''fifth IndieWebCamp in Nürnberg''', is planned for 2026-06-27…28 and [https://nuernberg.digital/de/events/2026/indiewebcamp-nuernberg-2026 part of the Nuremberg Digital Festival].

Add the event to your calendar!
* [https://events.indieweb.org/2026/06/-indiewebcamp-nuremberg-7EIKg0lqfg93 '''🗓 IndieWebCamp Nürnberg June 27-28, 2026''']<!--, '''10AM-5PM'''-->.

🎪 '''IndieWebCamp''' is a two-day collaborative workshop with interactive discussions, brainstorming, creating, hacking, and teaching & helping each other make more and better use of our personal websites for a more fulfilling experience on the web.

1️⃣ '''Day one, 27 June''' we’ll do introductions and facilitate discussions in a [[BarCamp]] like environment. Bring a topic you'd like to discuss or join on topics as they are added to the schedule grid.

2️⃣ '''Day two, 28 June''' is our [[Create Day]] when we make, design, build, and code what we discussed. Work in groups with others or on your own. We do demos at the end of the day for everyone to show off what they worked on and made!

See '''[[IndieWebCamps/Attending|Attending an IndieWebCamp]]''' for more details if this is your first time!

== Accessibility ==
Whatever your disability, we want to make sure IndieWebCamps are accessible to all. If you consider attending IndieWebCamp Nuremberg and have specific accessibility requirements, contact us in advance and we'll try to make sure that we are able to accommodate you.

== Health and Safety ==
The venue has plenty of space to sit distanced. <!-- checking into: and windows which we can and will open to provide fresh airflow.-->

If you are feeling sick or experiencing any respiratory (Covid, RSV, flu, cold) symptoms, we require that you please participate remotely instead of in-person.
{{emojicon|😷}}
Masking 😷 recommended (but not required) both in-person inside the venue, and in transit (airports, flights, trains) to the venue.

Plan to bring N95 (or equivalent) respirator or KN95 (or equivalent like FFP2) masks to wear.

Neither the venue nor the Nuremberg Digital Festival has any explicit health and safety rules, however we are recommending masking to help mitigate risks, both for IndieWebCamp participants, and for those we may interact with outside the camp.

Covid Rules are what the venue ([[tollwerk]]) required as of 2023 and are subject to change before the event.
