== Notes ==

* How to unblock yourself and actually post more often
** Post something that brings you joy, something you're doing right now
*** Why put what you're doing into a silo like Twitter when you can post your own status feed
** Check-ins on micro.blog site > streams to main site (?)
*** [[checkin]]
** [[Swarms]] 
** blog post versus [[note]]
** {{jo}} uses a terminal interface to post short notes, where it's not like writing a post in a IDE with paragraphs
*** [[note | notes]] are presented as [[h-feed]], not rss/xml
* {{capjamesg}} wants a personal view for himself
** homepage could show you a writing prompt
** to get into the writing mode, the first step is the hardest
*** "starting a new post is like lifting a 100kg weight"
*** "but as soon as I start I can't stop"

* section of ideas to write about?

* {{t}} has many ideas pop up and has a list of ideas that he goes to for inspiration occasionally
* Daniel posts a Polaroid every day -- a way to keep up a challenge to post
** https://polaroids.danielpietzsch.com/
** [[HWC]] short summaries, a short review of movies are common post types by Dan
*** Good mental reference, the material is fresh and nice to write
*** {{t}} feels the need to include links to the thing he's writing about
**** but feels that finding links bring you out of the flow

** writing about what is happening right now (like Twitter), what you experienced, what you're doing, what you saw (review), 
** vs writing more deep thoughts about something, thinking, analysis

* [[Blogtober]] and other challenges
** it's hard to keep up with those as sometimes there's nothing happening that day perhaps!
** Then it feels forced, not good quality

* some people in [[HWC]] say they feel bad not posting enough
** {{jo}} only posts when they have something to say

* Don't be sorry for the absence

* When you have something to say, say it!
 
* Word limits can be freeing 
** semi-related https://book.micro.blog/ui-impacts-behavior/
* Whole point of [[Twitter]], the character limit was freeing

* A post doesn't have to be the whole thing like it has to be linear or complete

* Might write a blog post about part of something. Then later write another blog post that builds on that. For a lot of things you can take incrementally on a blog

* Backlog of drafts of posts to finish.

* Some keep writing until a blog post is done otherwise you lose the spark.

* Q: When you get interrupted, how do you deal with the unfinished blog post?
** Even if it is really late, Jo finishes it or else she'll never go back to it
** Naty tries to finish it soon or else it also gets into a draft folder that doesn't get seen again

* Q: Daily blogging habit? Any specific time?
** {{jo}} - in the evenings usually, need 1 hr minimum 
** {{capjamesg}} - writes after a walk or in the evening as well

* Avoid writing like [[LinkedIn]]
** "I went on holiday and here are the five things I learned"

* Writing interface, that might stress you out to help you write 
** {{capjamesg}} "wrote a blog post before getting the train" - https://jamesg.blog/2025/04/13/morning-css

* {{chrisaldrich}} writes blog posts on a typewriter
** https://boffosocko.com/2026/04/17/on-holy-grail-typewriters/

* Jeremy wrote (on paper) a blog post before and OCR it into a blog post, because some people are not so good with typing
* In 2009, {{t}} discovered a stack of carbon copy post it notes in Austin, Texas
** Wrote date + status = analogue [[Twitter]]
** The post-it-notes would fly off eventually
** Therefore, just like [[tweet |tweets]] , they're ephemeral
