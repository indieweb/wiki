== Notes ==
*Home page design is important for when someone comes to your site.

* What kind of experience do you want your visitors to have?
** Less content to not overwhelm users
** You want people to keep reading, also want to be clear.
** Home page should be interesting so people keep reading and not get bored.
** Your home page should be fun and distinct.
* One pager to simplify things, but becomes very basic
* It's like a conversation, think of the human visiting the site and what you would tell them about yourself

* You wouldn't overwhelm someone with everything when you see them for the first time; the same is true of home pages too.
** James putting new things on the homepage, 1 in 1 out because it gets too long
* {{capjamesg}} is constantly editing his homepage
** _almost_ a one in one out policy

* Why would you care? (I couldn't catch the question Tantek asked)
** How to attract or keep readers after clicking into your site

* {{jo}} purposely decided not to use button-style links
** "I want my website to feel calmer than that"
** Jo wants the site to feel fun, expresses the vibe via the design and interaction with the site content
** uses the open heart protocol service hosted by https://benji.dog
*** https://openheart.fyi/
*** https://www.benji.dog/articles/interactions-or-reactions/
*** Naty uses something similar by iine and supabase records the likes (she wrote a guide https://burgeonlab.com/blog/add-appreciation-buttons-to-hugo-with-iine/) which can be self hosted)

* Homepage communicates the vibes of the person they want to show to other people

* Fun interactions likes, buttons can keep a reader on the site
* But can be distracting for the actual content
* Need a good balance between the two

* For personal sites, creating interactions can display your skills
** It's a way of expressing yourself via site design

* {{capjamesg}} uses blue because it's the colour of peace
** Mention of homepage that is a videogame
*** "I was lost in the forest.. literally"
* Sometimes something familiar is nice
** Text-focused vs colourful
* Designing the site is like decorating your room

* Interactions as social-context

* Q: Do you link to everything on your home page or rely on navigation only?
** Jo knows some have homepages that are fills the whole screen
** showing last 10 blog posts vs 30
** James would enjoy seeing a photo gallery on the homepage as it is colourful
** Jo doesn't want art to clog up the site, but it does require the user to scroll down
** Anchor links / jump links can direct users to important bits that are below the inital screen

* Q: Is everyone happy with their homepage right now?
** Resounding No from everyone not happy with their homepage
** remote participants agree with that
*** Naty -- yes I want to improve

* Q: Who is designing their site mobile-first?
** Most people probably use their phone when looking at or discovering your website
** People here seem to be more desktop-first oriented
** How much can you fit on the mobile experience?

* Mobile phones disrupted the "pixel-perfect" approach to design
** Filtering the most important information to show on small screens, expanding the amount of information with bigger screens
* Optimize for mobile first approach
** Top-to-bottom approach changed to bottom-to-top approach
*** in regards to screen size

* {{jo}}'s site is all one column, so it only shows one piece of content at a time
** makes you consider content priority 
*** As it's a personal site, you can display the information depending on your character
*** eg. retro, messy, strict structure
** Two column themes on desktop can be swiped left or right to see
*** Navigation mode using scroll-snap is a new CSS

**** dispels the notion that people have to stay in those apps to get that experience
