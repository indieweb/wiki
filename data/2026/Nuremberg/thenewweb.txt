== Notes ==
* Nora: recently went to an event where someone talked about transforming website for people who are used to AI. They created a chat bot that uses AI that they are selling to companies that they can integrate into their websites as a HTML element / JS embeds.
** Changes the way websites are being perceived.
** The way we perceive websites has changed and will change in the future.
** Worrying. Looking at all the cool websites, the vintage websites, there is a potential in the future wher websites like that will stop existing or stop being perceived like that.
** You might only talk about the content because you are using a text-based tool like ChatGPT which reduces a website into text and doesn't appreciate the visuals.
** Creativity will be reduced or cease to exist as LLMs only care about content
** Visual aspects and the fun in web might disappear
** How do we maintain the fun aspects of the web?
** Being yourself on the web will attract readers to visit your site
** Do your own thing, especially if you're an artist, express yourself
** If browsers will still exist in five to ten years from now
** You need a browser to consume websites
** "the web replaced books, yet I read more books now"
** Feed readers didn't replace the web, social media didn't replace the web
** Many tools said they would replace the web but it didn't manifests
** I can give you a link to a timetable I found for a certain day for trains, like NUR to BER trains, you can't from an app.
** It should "just" be a technical problem to share information, but we haven't found anything better than links. People do share screenshots. But if the information changes.
** As long as dynamic of humans sharing exists, and you want to get the latest version, as long as that is true, you will have web browses.
** Apps are locked in, which result in people sharing screenshots
** Rene building corporate web apps, less native apps get developed
* Web apps are being more popular now
** So many "native apps" use Electron, like Slack, Discord, etc. Electron uses Chrome.
*** "the web" is the APIs that power those apps
* The modern vision that we don't need UIs is *a* vision, but we can't imagine replacing software used on a daily basis with a generic/on-the-fly generated UI.
* the science of information architecture design
** https://omg.lol and https://neocities.org
* 700k people use Neocities, omg.lol runs thousands of sites -- these are each an individual's representation of creativity.
* James' website is half of his creative life. Neocities is 700k people's creativity.
* The university James studies at online
* Snapchat only supports in-app browser for opening links
** In-app web views can break a lot
** Social media in-app attending is difficult
** People can still share links from a web view
* link usage is different for [normies] than for us IndieWebbers
* Opening an app first rather than opening a website
** IG links are loud: open in app, have to click continuing on the web, just to watch the funny cat video on the browser. Purposefully restricting information.

* Two UK examples
** https://gov.uk
*** Someone only had access to a Sony PSP to access the internet
**** https://shkspr.mobi/blog/2021/01/the-unreasonable-effectiveness-of-simple-html/
** BBC, NYT
* How to support critical work without paywalling information 
* About archiving
** https://archive.is archives without the paywall: news articles are archivable, but there are hoops
** The more information goes private, the less we can see without an account / private.
* Articles that misrepresent information
** If you only read the title, you might only get half the picture.
** Operating on fragments of information.
* April: living on the internet and getting to know the internet
** Taking intentional steps about how to consume information 
** Change "media diet" from IG and social media to finding new websites, exploring different indie websites, since last year, adding different people's links to prevent a dead-end
** The IndieWeb doesn't have a dead end: links help with discoverability
** We need to revive the culture of the web
** Kagi (customized search engines) is good to discover indie / non-corporate sites
** Neocities allows for easier discovery 
* Being opinionated / intentional about how we look for information
* Gen Z shouldn't accept the mainstream and should keep exploring instead of just scrolling on social media
* We underestimate how dependent people are on GPT/Claude, etc., even things that would previously require a Google Search.
** Even looking up translations that you can do using a browser, they use ChatGPT.
** It can be depressing to think about that.
** The AI misses the personality from what you made.
* How to convince artists to join the web?
* Can help them get started by suggesting services but it needs their continued motivation to keep creating on their own site
* Most people value convenience
* It's hard to have a moral discussion about tech because it can feel accusatory (is this the right word?)
* There are people who question "Who will know / connect with me?"
* "How do I talk to my friends on there?"
* James doesn't have the words to convince someone without a pre-existing interest to start a website
* Lurkers
** Those who only consume
** Related: https://www.ofcom.org.uk/online-safety/protecting-children/younger-phone-owners-the-rise-of-ai-and-consumption-over-creation-our-latest-look-at-uk-childrens-media-lives
** "Two‑thirds (65%) of 8-17 year olds who use social media say they mainly read, watch, follow or ‘like’ content on social media platforms, compared to around a third (34%) who say they share"
* Using social media as a mobile TV
* Social media is just there; it's easy to pick up and participate.
* No motivation to share something to the world, just consuming from scrolling
* No desire to actually informed about something specific - but just being 'fed' with whatever information is being served
* Without tending to the Youtube algorithm, the suggested content is very "meh" 
* For some people the solution is touching grass :)
* Maybe lurkers have a healthier relationship with consumption? 
* Chilling effect of the long-term impacts of what you publish
** Especially if you have to attach your legal name / address in terms of having an Impressum
* Diary app
* https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_Diary
* Stumbling onto great content
** "the neocities effect"
* tension on the web that certain mediums feel "more important" than others
** a long documentatry video feels "more serious"
** we're missing a good ontology on the web
*** serious
*** ephemeral
* Vloggers do what bloggers do: sharing their lives (albeit with different mediums)
* People listen to podcasts because of the people that make it, it's not just the content
** AI summaries can't really replace them
* mention of creating a Homebrew Website Club podcast
** written mention of https://web-weaving.jamesg.blog/
* We all long for humanity rather than robots
