* What has changed in publishing over the last 20 years?
* Searching knowledge graphs?

* How do digital gardens fit in?
* So many pieces of knowledge to document
** Christian's examples: AI history, ethics, etc.
* What is the right way to present knowledge on the web?
* knowledge now is multi-modal
** everyone's used to interacting differently
** Downside: it's hard to know what modality to begin on that isn't informed mainly by what everyone else is doing
*** audience knows which form fits them best
** moved on from blogs to social media, and we're reduced to 200 characters (on X etc.)
* blogging
** has the original publish date
** posts can have sequels
** versus notes that are updated constantly
*** and can be more free-form
* There are lots of courses for learning today
* There are many channels for sharing information
** video can become podcast which can become text
* Where would you start to build new knowledge?
** On your own website!
** Using your personal notes (i.e. Apple Notes)
** Having separate areas for different hobbies
** Blog posts can build on each other
* https://indieweb.org/digital_garden
* Modern techniques allow for so many different kinds of distribution
** Universal Design for Learning framework: creating content in so many different ways that people can choose their own preferences
** Relates to audio/kinaesthetic/visual learning styles
* Where to start: text-based to get an idea written down, which is a starting point for building knowledge
** Starting with a website with text, pictures, etc.
** WP is a place to start for a blog, but is there an equivalent tech stack for digital gardens?
*** Notion, Roam Research

** able to see how the parts of your garden grows
** The benefits of being able to visualise what you're building
** How can a digital garden encourage you to create more connections between topics
* there might be a lack of a built-in audience
* Social media algorithms change, which means it's hard to build something long-term and sustainable there
* audience of a digital garden, writing for yourself vs others
** humans vs algorithms
** Doing something for you; if it happens to interest someone else, that is fine too

** The content all starts from your thoughts
** The more metadata you have, the more connections you can create
