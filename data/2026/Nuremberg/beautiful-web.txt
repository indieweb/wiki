== Notes ==
* somewhat a reaction to design phenomena like:
** https://www.theverge.com/news/684321/no-more-liquid-ass
* A tool from Germany: https://vogel.haus
** But name is not memorable
** Coporate website nowadays look all shiny and generic

* Agricultural tool: https://esd-systeme.de
* An opposite website, a vintage looking page

* Rene worked on a portfolio site with the help of LLM, but feels a bit generic

* https://spaceburger.eu
** A cool restaurant site that looks very retro
** Readable pixel font, with good line-spacing

* https://ratfactor.com
** polygonal artwork of a rat in a rainbow colorway

* https://artifactor.n2.studio/v2/
* Generates web art every 15 seconds, using generative art makes it interactive and fresh on every visit
** By the same person who did https://n2.studio/work/beyond-tellerrand-tellerrandomizer
** Uses Kirby
** Used in-between Beyond Tellerrand talks to display quotes and images from the previous talk

* https://renebreckner.de/posts/cool-websites
** Rene's collection of cool sites

* https://lushprojects.com
** A 20+ year blogger sharing projects with many designs for each post

* Rene wants creative and ever-changing websites that don't look like your typical 'Apple product page' copy

* Neocities have a grid of websites that are all unique and creative
** Sidebars, buttons, boxes?

* Q: Is it able to create a "retro" site but still be mobile-friendly and user-friendly/accessible?
** Retro sites commonly have a message that it doesn't work well on mobile devices
* Can modern technologies be used but appear retro?
** Not rounded corners for everything
* There are window 98 CSS themes for example, https://github.com/jdan/98.css/
* Reminded of Tumblr websites

* Web design index books
* Sites back in the day each had their own flair/uniqueness
** But modern tools made everyone's site the same looking
* https://indieweb.org/design

* https://www.kickscondor.com/
** Paralax scrolling and background changes

* Jo remembers a site that simulates a VHS tape 
** https://robophobia.org/videos

* https://ohhelloana.blog/
** Ana is in one of the episodes of James's podcast, https://web-weaving.jamesg.blog/7
* Nice grid paper background

* https://jasonsantamaria.com
** Modern looking site, but at the bottom there is previous version archives
** Previous pages are designed per article

* https://lakeside-boydyke-camping.neocities.org
** Retro cursor

* https://henry.codes/writing 
** Print design, stylistic typography, with images

* https://anne-katharina.com/work
** Simulating windows, resize each image/video
** It remembers what was closed and position

* https://www.art.yale.edu/
** Yale School of Art
** Repeated image background
** Retro design

* "When you think in print, it's always easier to think a bit crazier in things like crazy fonts"
* Zines are a good source of inspiration, not other websites

* Rene created artwork with Affinity for free
* Creating art like this makes Rene happy

* https://adbk-nuernberg.de/
** Typography is not typical coporate style
** Images have retro vibes

* https://hfg-offenbach.de

* Seeing the web like how artist seeing his paint and tools
* There are implicit templates on how to do things
* Start with a framework
* Web as a Medium
** Web can be curated like for e.g. we use paint, brush and artistic stuff to create an experience.
