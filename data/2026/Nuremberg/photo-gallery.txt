== Notes ==
* Some people already have galleries 
** Hard to research good resources to display photographs on a personal site
* Layouts:
** Grid + enlagrge
** Slideshow
** Lightbox
** Carousel style like IG
* Post monthly posts with 100s of photos per month
** Overview page for months, and ordered by year
** Photos can be linked with fragment links
* {{daniel}} demoes his photos sites (https://photojournal.danielpietzsch.com/ and https://polaroids.danielpietzsch.com/)
** Polaroids have all the same aspect ration, easy to display in a grid
** Responsive flex-box css to fill the width of the window
* {{maxhaesslein}}: https://www.maxhaesslein.de/visual/photography/moments/
** Photo diary sorted by year
** based on Kirby, picture element that grabs all the images from a folder
** `max-height` and `max-width` to prevent cropping of images, will see whole image all the time no matter the size of device/window 
** Use arrow keys for navigation, picture always gets centered during navigation
** Camera info is displayed when hovering over the image
** Hash link next to the image so deep links are possible (no direct URLs to every image though)
** Having the galleries on the public site means it is already curated, which is used for annual photobooks
* {{t}}
** started posting individual photos, gallery came later in the sidebar showing recent photos on the homepage
** Clicking on a thumbnail, goes to the page with single (or multiple images), then at the bottom of the collection there's a caption/blog post section about the photos
* https://tantek.com/photos is a separate page with a grid/cropped view and is responsive
** This is like an index page of all posts that are photo posts
** Mimics Instagram grid view
** Every photo post has its own URL
** Wants to add prev/next navigation for individual photo URLs that go to the prev/next photo post
* René wrote a report for a friend about structures of photo displays on the web
** Collage style, found chaotic and other (sometimes provocative) styles
** Example 1: https://guillaumetomasi.com 
*** Photos are hidden and only shown when hovering over like a special viewing glass
** Example 2: https://bridget.pictures
*** Photos appear on cursor movement on desktop and on mobile, photos come up as you scroll
** Most galleries can click through with arrows and organized in project folders
* Example 3: https://porodina.com
** Photobook approach, need to curate which photo pairs well together as they appear as two together
* Wants a way to arrange photos artistically for a stylistic presentation
* Click an image to see enlarged version
* Images should have an permalink so they can be shared easily
* Solution should be as low friction as possible ("IndieWeb-style")
* {{burgeonlab}} has built on Hugo a photos page also with a 3 column grid but only has three photos at the moment, https://burgeonlab.com/photos/
** Multiple photos per post is supported with a CSS-only carousel design
** Wants to work on full-bleed mobile version and one day import IG archive
** Navigation at the bottom is limited to only photo posts so it will be in order of the index page

* Aspect ratios - fix it to three for example, and set rules for each to work in a grid layout 
** Masonary layout for CSS is available https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Guides/Grid_layout/Masonry_layout like Flickr's layout (still experimental and not supported on all browsers)

* Importance of image loading performance
** Very small JPG embedded in HTML to help speed up loading
** Image formats
** Lazy loading the correct image size depending on your device size

* Q: How does the viewing device size affect the content?
** If it's mobile, Tantek recommends full-bleed to utilize every bit of screen estate
** Daniel suggests do not disable zoom, allow pinch to zoom on mobile

* Want to show metadata of photos on hover, but not sure how to present it yet - maybe some icons?
* Want to have improved desktop layout that is more focused on creativity/expression, e.g. grouping of photos, alignment on a page, pairing images and maybe not just chronologically ordered or a random dump

* Methods on displaying photos on one's site
** Galleries - groups of photos
*** https://indieweb.org/gallery
** Grid view - a lot of people chose a grid of width 3 to display photos (inspired by Instagram)
*** https://indieweb.org/photo_grid
** Recent photos - just a minimal entry point to full photo content
*** https://indieweb.org/recent_photos
** Photos stream - like grid, but expanded with caption and date inline
*** https://indieweb.org/photo_stream
