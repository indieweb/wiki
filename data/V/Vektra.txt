<dfn>Vektra</dfn> makes a log management SaaS called Current. http://current.sh

Previously, Vektra was building a (largely) Heroku-compatible platform as a service (PaaS) that can be run locally on your own computer or "in the cloud" on a server. It planned to be compatible most Heroku build packs which are what provides support for languages and frameworks ([https://github.com/heroku/heroku-buildpack-ruby Ruby], [https://github.com/heroku/heroku-buildpack-nodejs NodeJS], [https://github.com/heroku/heroku-buildpack-java Java], [https://github.com/heroku/heroku-buildpack-play Play], [https://github.com/heroku/heroku-buildpack-python Python], [https://github.com/heroku/heroku-buildpack-php PHP], [https://github.com/heroku/heroku-buildpack-clojure Clojure], [https://github.com/kr/heroku-buildpack-go Go], [https://github.com/oortcloud/heroku-buildpack-meteorite Meteorite], [https://github.com/miyagawa/heroku-buildpack-perl Perl], [https://github.com/heroku/heroku-buildpack-scala Scala], [https://github.com/igrigorik/heroku-buildpack-dart Dart], [https://github.com/rhy-jot/buildpack-nginx Nginx], [https://github.com/Kloadut/heroku-buildpack-static-apache Apache]).

The company is made up of Evan Phoenix, Kristen Reyes and [[User:Jlsuttles.me|Jessica Suttles]].

See also: [http://vektra.com vektra.com].
