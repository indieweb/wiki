To '''<dfn>brigade</dfn>''' or '''<dfn>brigading</dfn>''' is a coordinated effort by an online group to bully, manipulate, or harass an individual or another group (e.g. by mass [[comment|commenting]], [[repost|reposting]], or [[like|liking]]).

Recommendation: [[user-interface]] features which provide the ability to enable abuse like brigading should generally be avoided in web interactions and designs.

== Examples ==
=== Gamergate ===
<blockquote>As Gamergate unfolded, Wetherell noticed its participants were using the retweet to “brigade,” or coordinate their attacks against their targets, disseminating misinformation and outrage at a pace that made it difficult to fight back. The [https://medium.com/message/72-hours-of-gamergate-e00513f7cf5d retweet button propelled Gamergate], according to an analysis by the technologist and blogger Andy Baio. In his study of 316,669 Gamergate tweets sent over 72 hours, 217,384 were retweets, or about 69%.&mdash;''[https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/alexkantrowitz/how-the-retweet-ruined-the-internet The Man Who Built The Retweet: “We Handed A Loaded Weapon To 4-Year-Olds”]'' via BuzzFeed News</blockquote>


== IndieWeb (counter) examples ==
* [[micro.blog]] creator {{manton}} has stated that he's designed his platform to specifically not have a repost/retweet functionality as a means of helping to prevent abuse.
