'''<dfn>[https://bubbles.town bubbles.town]</dfn>''' is an [[aggregator]] service for personal blogs that allows people to upvote posts, similar to [[Hacker News]] or [[Lobsters]].

== Criticism ==
=== Use of LLMs ===
2026-04-23: [https://gamedev.lgbt/@renkotsuban/116452969099340536 via Mastodon], the labeling of posts was being done with the help of AI.

In an interview with {{brennan}}, the developer confirmed and apologized for the initial use of AI, and that data no longer leaves the server:
* {{citation
| title = Floating Up: An Interview with the creator of Bubbles.town, Benjamin Behnke
| url = https://brennan.day/floating-up-an-interview-with-the-creator-of-bubbles-town-benjamin-behnke/#bens-response
| published = 2026-04-27 
| author = {{brennan}}
| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20260608185524/https://brennan.day/floating-up-an-interview-with-the-creator-of-bubbles-town-benjamin-behnke/#bens-response
}}

<blockquote>"There are no API calls to Anthropic, OpenAI, or any other AI provider from the Bubbles server today. Categorisation runs through a small local Naive Bayes model that I have trained on the categorisation Claude did previously. That took some time to set up. The trained vocabulary is published at bubbles.town/classifier/model_en.json and bubbles.town/classifier/model_de.json so anyone can download it and see exactly what the classifier knows. Since yesterday, no data leaves the Bubbles server!"</blockquote>
