A '''<dfn>blog carnival</dfn>''' is a form of independent, personally curated content aggregation, often hosted by different people in rotation, in which the host would write a post linking to any posts submitted on the chosen topic.

== IndieWeb Examples ==
=== Jeremy Cherfas ===
{{jeremycherfas}} has quite a long history with [https://stream.jeremycherfas.net/2021/looking-at-an-old-post-of-mine blog carnivals]. He hosted some back in the day and lately has tried to revive the idea by [https://www.jeremycherfas.net/blog/weeding-mendels-garden-and-other-blog-carnivals revisiting old posts] and, indeed, [https://www.jeremycherfas.net/blog/looking-for-todays-blog-carnivals searching for the remnants of carnival culture]. A search for Carnival on his site will turn up other goodies.

=== Sara Jak≈°a ===
{{sarajaksa}} started the [[indieweb-carnival|IndieWeb carnival]] here. She also hosted an edition of the [https://carnivalofaros.wordpress.com/  A Carnival of Aros] and participated in [https://asexualagenda.wordpress.com/a-carnival-of-aces-masterpost/ A Carnival of Aces] and [https://the.artocalypse.org/upcoming-blog-carnivals/ Artocalypse blog carnival] by writing on her blog.
