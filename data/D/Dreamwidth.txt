'''<dfn>[https://www.dreamwidth.org Dreamwidth]</dfn>''' is an [[open source]]<ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/site/opensource Dreamwidth: Open Source]</ref><ref>[https://github.com/dreamwidth/ Dreamwidth Studios GitHub]</ref> social networking and [[blog|blogging]] site based on [[LiveJournal]] code and committed to open access, [[interoperability]], and respecting user [[privacy]].<ref name ="principles">[https://www.dreamwidth.org/legal/principles Dreamwidth Guiding Principles]</ref><ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqid=1 Dreamwidth FAQ: What is Dreamwidth Studios?]</ref> It was originally launched in 2009.<ref>[https://dw-news.dreamwidth.org/5035.html Welcome to Dreamwidth Studios!]</ref>

Dreamwidth has played a role in actively combating legal challenges to user privacy on the internet.<ref>[https://dw-advocacy.dreamwidth.org/260.html The Current List of Cases]</ref><ref>[https://dw-news.dreamwidth.org/43389.html In which we level up our fight for your digital civil rights]</ref><ref>[https://dw-news.dreamwidth.org/43997.html In which we update you about our lawsuits, and welcome a wave of new friends!]</ref> In partnership with NetChoice, Dreamwidth staff have participated in cases such as  NetChoice v. Skrmetti<ref>[https://dw-advocacy.dreamwidth.org/514.html Netchoice v Skrmetti (TN)]</ref> and Netchoice, LLC v Yost.<ref>[https://dw-news.dreamwidth.org/43698.html In which we help to sue Ohio]</ref>

Dreamwidth Studios, LLC operates on a user-funded [[business model]]. Users can support the site by purchasing extra features for their accounts or gifting paid features to others.<ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqid=233 Dreamwidth FAQ: What is a paid/premium paid/seed account?]</ref><ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqid=4 Dreamwidth FAQ: What are the extra services available?]</ref><ref>[https://shop.dreamwidth.org/account?for=gift Buy a Paid Account]</ref> Dreamwidth does not accept or display third-party advertising.<ref name="principles" /><ref>[https://synecdochic.dreamwidth.org/221888.html#:~:text=advertising Announcing Dreamwidth Studios]</ref>

Dreamwidth's site policies on 18+ content<ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqid=147 FAQ: Adult Content]</ref> meant the site attracted some attention as a [[Tumblr]] alternative in the wake of the 2018 Tumblr porn ban.<ref>[https://www.wired.com/story/tumblr-porn-bloggers-dreamwidth-pillowfort/ Tumblr's Displaced Porn Bloggers Test Their New Platforms]</ref>

* [[private post|Private posts]]<ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqcat=rels Dreamwidth FAQ: Relationships]</ref>

* [[moderation|Comment screening]]<ref>[Dreamwidth FAQ: Comments https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqcat=badcomments]</ref>

* [[HTML]] and rich text options for [[posts|post]] formatting<ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqid=155 Dreamwidth FAQ: What HTML tags can I use on Dreamwidth?]</ref>

* [[RSS]] and [[Atom]] feeds generated for all accounts<ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqid=117&q=rss&lang= Dreamwidth FAQ: Can I get an RSS or Atom feed of my Dreamwidth journal?]</ref> 

* RSS [[feed reader]] capability<ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/feeds/ Dreamwidth: Feeds]</ref><ref>[https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqid=27&q=rss&lang=  Dreamwidth FAQ: How do I create or subscribe to a feed (RSS/Atom)?]</ref>
